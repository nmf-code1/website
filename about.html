History & Heritage

Commissioned in the 19th century to protect the Royal Dockyard at Portsmouth, No Man's Fort now stands as a striking heritage landmark. Constructed with granite walls up to fifteen feet thick, the fort once housed heavy guns guarding the Solent. Today, it has been carefully converted into a luxury venue while retaining its historic character.

The Fort Today

Spanning four floors and approximately 99,000 square feet, the fort offers 23 en-suite bedrooms, a helipad, and private jetties. Its unique circular layout surrounds an open courtyard and panoramic rooftop decks.
